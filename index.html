Research Interests

Prof. Seena Dehkharghani's research interests include the translational use of neuroimaging for biomarkers of hemodynamic stress, metabolism, and tissue viability.

  • Computed Tomography Perfusion to Predict Response to Recanalization in Ischemic Stroke Project, or CRISP
  • Solitaire™ with the Intention for Thrombectomy as Primary Endovascular Treatment for Acute Ischemic Stroke, or SWIFT-PRIME
  • Diffusion Weighted Imaging or Computerized Tomography Perfusion Assessment with Clinical Mismatch in the Triage of Wake Up and Late Presenting Strokes Undergoing Neurointervention, or DAWN

NYU Langone Radiology — Center for Biomedical Imaging

NYU Langone radiology research instrumentation includes multiple leading-edge imaging systems, a research-only MRI test bay, a state-of-the-art radiofrequency (RF) engineering laboratory, a radiochemistry facility, and high-performance computing clusters.

Center for Advanced Imaging Innovation and Research (CAI2R)

CAI2R research is primarily focused on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI).

CAI2R also explore positron emission tomography (PET), computed tomography (CT), and ultrasound (US), alone or in combination with MRI.

Open Positions

Are you interested in joining our team at the Laboratory of Bioelectromagnetics and Imaging Neuroscience at NYU?

We have openings for various positions, including postdocs, PhD students, and high school students.

Potential Postdoc, PhD, visiting students, and high-school students

  • Postdoctoral Researchers

    If you're considering a postdoc position with us, please send your CV, homepage, research objectives, and other relevant documents to us. We look forward to reviewing your application and potentially welcoming you to our team.

  • PhD Students

    If you're applying to study at NYU and are interested in collaborating with us, we encourage you to apply for the PhD program. Declare a specialization in areas such as Computer Vision, Imaging, Machine Learning, or Radiology. In your application, be sure to mention our lab and your specific interests. We eagerly anticipate meeting you upon your admission.

  • High School Students

    If you're a high school student in the NJ / NYC area and have an interest in research, please don't hesitate to contact us directly.

NYU students

  • Graduate Students

    If you're a graduate student at NYU and wish to join or collaborate with our lab, send an email that includes a brief description of your research interests and a link to your homepage. We'll arrange a meeting to discuss potential collaborations.

  • Undergraduate Students

    If you're a NYU undergraduate interested in research, please email us your CV or resume, a recent transcript, and a brief description of your desired projects. We're open to advising students from all departments.

We look forward to receiving applications from dedicated individuals who share our passion for research and innovation!

Apply by sending your CV, cover letter and list of references by email to Seena.Dehkharghani@nyulangone.org and Leeor.Alon@nyulangone.org
